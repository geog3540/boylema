Introduction to Geovisualization Portfolio GEOG 5540

Choropleth map: MN Percent Poverty

This map demonstrates a heterogenous spatial distribution of percent poverty across the entire state of Minnesota. The most noticeable pattern is the ring of light purple, representing the lowest percentage of poverty, around the twin cities. This is reasonable since that area is mainly comprised of suburbs that house family units with higher income levels due to higher housing prices and the number of quality school districts present. Percent poverty is used as a environmental justice variable, and the relationship between it and the density of CAFOs (concentrated animal feeding operations) in each census tract would be interesting to see. This relationship would most likely be strongest outside major city limits

Proportional Symbol map: Over 300lb hog counts in MN CAFOs

This map visualizes the CAFOs (concentrated animal feeding operations) in Minnesota that are documented to house hogs that are greater than 300lbs. The majority of these CAFOs are located on the South and Southwest edge of the state, which is reasonable since these are more rural areas. The numbers of hogs at each CAFO does vary evenly thoughout the distribution of CAFOs. With many smaller CAFOs with numbers under 1k located amongst much larger CAFOs with numbers close to 10k, ranging all the way up to 28k.

Bivariate Choropleth map: Percent poverty vs. Percent non-English

In this map, areas of high poverty and high percentage of non-English speakers are shown as dark orange and areas of low poverty and low percentage of non-English speakers are shown as white. There are some pockets of high/high areas in the more rural parts of Minnesota, however the majority of the rural parts of the state are more yellow or a dull purple, suggesting high poverty and low percent non-English speakers or medium levels of each attribute. The more interesting patterns may lie closer the the urban area of St.Paul/Minnesota. There is a halo of white (low/low) census tracts surrounding the city. The census tracts within the city center show areas of dark orange, representing areas of high poverty and high percentage of non-English speakers. Some of these high/high tracts are next to areas of purple representing still high percentages of non-English speakers, but lower poverty levels.
